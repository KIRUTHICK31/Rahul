What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease where cells in the breast grow out of control, forming a tumor that can often be seen on an x-ray or felt as a lump. It is the most common cancer diagnosed in women worldwide, though it can also occur in men. The cancer usually starts in either the lobules or the ducts of the breast, which are the glands that produce milk and the pathways that bring milk to the nipple, respectively.

How is it Caused?

Breast cancer is caused by a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. While specific causes can vary, common risk factors include age, a family history of breast cancer, inherited genetic mutations such as BRCA1 and BRCA2, prolonged exposure to estrogen, and lifestyle choices such as alcohol consumption, obesity, and lack of physical activity. Not all causes are preventable, and having one or more risk factors does not mean a person will definitely develop the disease.

Prevention

While not all breast cancers can be prevented, there are several steps individuals can take to significantly reduce their risk. These include maintaining a healthy weight, engaging in regular physical activity, limiting alcohol intake, and avoiding smoking. Regular screening and mammograms can also help detect the disease in its early stages when it is most treatable. For those at high genetic risk, preventive surgeries or medications might also be an option.
